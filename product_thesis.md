this is my product scope! 

aster is a multimodal mental-health copilot for reflection, early warning, and safe escalation

product definition:
aster is a privacy-conscious mental-health support app that helps users reflect through text and voice journaling, notice patterns early through behavioral and emotional signals, regulate in difficult moments with guided interventions, and escalate safely when risk rises.

the problem:
I've always struggled with anxiety, but I didn't even realize I had it until it escaled to a severe anxiety attack episode, which led me to take a medical leave. During my leave, I consulted a therapist, but because of her busy schedule, she wasn't always avaiable, so I tried talking to large language models, which led to a disastrous outcome. I realised my mistake, talked to my theraapist and we decided to try various apps. After much experimentation and no useful help, I realized that most mental-health apps fall into one of these buckets:
1) generic mood trackers
2) content libraries with low engagement
3) therapy chatbots that feel shallow or unsafe
4) wellness apps that do not personalize over time
Users often struggle not because they lack content, but because they lack:
1) timely self-awareness
2) continuity across days and weeks
3) structured reflection
4) support in the moment
5) safe next steps when distress rises
A person may not realize that their anxiety spikes after poor sleep, that isolation predicts their low mood, or that late-night rumination tends to precede emotional crashes. Existing apps rarely connect those dots in a way that feels personal, useful, and safe.

my insight: 
people do not need another 'AI therapist', an idea that is extremely unsafe. 
Tahey need a system that:
1) understands their day-to-day patterns
2) remembers what has been happening over time
3) helps them reflect more clearly
4) offers small, appropriate support in the moment
5) knows when not to act like a therapist
6) escalates safely when needed
The value is not in chatting. The value is in longitudinal intelligence and multimodal context and safety-aware action. 

product vision:
aster is the intelligence layer between daily life and formal care. It supports users between therapy sessions, before a crisis builds, and during ordinary stressful days when they need structure, reflection, and grounding.
It does not replace clinicians.
It improves:
1) self-awareness
2) early warning
3) adherence to healthy routines
4) handoff quality to trusted humans or professionals

target users for v1:
primary user: University students and young professionals
who experience stress, anxiety, burnout, loneliness, mood instability, or emotional overwhelm and want a structured, intelligent, nonjudgmental support system.
why this segment:
1) high stress and emotional variability
2) fragmented routines
3) sleep disruption is common
4) journaling and self-reflection are relevant
5) strong need for between-session support

what Aster does:
It helps you:
1) check in quickly
2) journal by text or voice
3) understand what may be driving your mood
4) get a useful support action in the moment
5) see weekly patterns
6) get safer escalation when distress becomes serious

what Aster is not:
1) a licensed therapist
2) a diagnostic tool
3) a medication advisor
4) a crisis hotline replacement
5) an autonomous mental-health authority
This boundary is central to the product. 

V1 product scope:
Included in V1:
1) onboarding with consent and preferences
2) daily mood/stress/energy/sleep check-ins
3) text journaling
4) voice journaling
5) AI summaries of journal content
6) extraction of emotions, triggers, and patterns
7) basic weekly insight report
8) guided interventions:
    a. breathing
    b. grounding
    c. CBT-style reframing
    d. sleep support
    e. behavioral activation
9) safety layer for high-risk language
10) trusted contact/crisis resource flow
11) longitudinal memory of themes and helpful interventions
Excluded from V1:
1) diagnosis
2) clinician dashboard
3) therapist note export
4) formal clinical scoring as product core
5) camera-based emotion inference
6) passive surveillance of private messages
7) medication recommendations
8) insurance/employer workflows

why Aster is different:
Most apps are either:
1) passive trackers
2) content warehouses
3) superficial chatbots

Aster is different because it combines:
1) text
2) voice
3) behavioral patterns
4) memory over time
5) agentic decision-making
6) safety-aware escalation
This means the app can move from:
“Here is generic wellness content” to “Your stress seems to rise after low-sleep evenings and your voice entries sound more tense when academic pressure builds. Here is the best next support action right now.” 

product loops:
1) Reflection loop: User journals → Aster extracts themes/emotions/triggers → asks one useful follow-up → user gains clarity
2) Support loop: User reports stress/distress → Aster recommends targeted intervention → user completes it → gives feedback → system personalizes
3) Insight loop: User logs check-ins/journals over time → Aster detects patterns → weekly insight improves self-awareness
4) Safety loop: User shows high-risk distress language → Aster stops generic flow → provides supportive response + resources + escalation options

user value proposition:
1) Immediate value
    “I can quickly unload what I’m feeling.”
    “I get help understanding what is going on.”
    “I get something concrete to do when overwhelmed.”
2) Medium-term value
    “I can see patterns in my stress, mood, and routines.”
    “The app feels more tailored to me over time.”
3) Long-term value
    “I catch downward spirals earlier.”
    “I have better continuity between difficult moments and actual care.”

'A therapy companion and student stress copilot'

    has a credible path to broader infrastructure later
2) Future expansion can include:
    therapist-facing summaries

    relapse monitoring
    university mental-health infrastructure
    employer burnout support

Aster should always feel:
1. Calm: No overwhelming UI, no robotic urgency unless safety truly requires it.
2. Respectful: The app should not sound controlling, invasive, or falsely certain.
3. Supportive, not performative: It should not overtalk, overanalyze, or pretend to know everything.
4. Safe: High-risk scenarios must trigger conservative, supportive behavior.
5. Personal over time: The system should remember usefully, but never feel creepy.
6. Actionable: Every meaningful interaction should leave the user with:
    insight
    emotional clarity
    or a concrete next step

product thesis:
Aster is not trying to replace therapy. It is building intelligent mental-health infrastructure for everyday life: a system that helps users reflect better, notice risk earlier, regulate in the moment, and connect more safely to human support when needed.
